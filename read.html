The Thaw Begins

The silence of the manor was not empty; it was heavy, pressed down by the weight of centuries of snow. Elara stood by the frosted window, her breath misting against the glass, watching the white expanse of the courtyard. For the first time in weeks, the storm had broken. A pale, watery sun struggled against the grey clouds, casting long, anaemic shadows across the drifts.

"It's melting," she whispered, though there was no one there to hear her. The statement felt like a transgression, a challenge to the frozen entity that held the house in its grip.

She turned away from the window, the floorboards groaning beneath her feet. The sound echoed through the cavernous hallway, a reminder of her isolation. She had come here seeking solitude, an escape from the noise of the city, but she had found only a deafening quiet that seemed to amplify her own thoughts until they were shouting.

The library was her sanctuary. Walls lined with books that smelled of dust and old paper, a scent that grounded her. She walked to the heavy oak desk in the center of the room, running her fingers over the carved surface. Here, at least, the cold seemed less intrusive.
